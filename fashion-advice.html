Fashion Advice & Style Tips

  • Emphasize the waist with belted dresses, fitted tops, and high-waisted skirts or pants.
  • Wrap dresses and bodycon styles are great for showing off your curves.
  • Avoid overly boxy or shapeless clothing that hides your natural proportions.
  • Empire waist dresses and tops create a more defined waistline.
  • Flowing fabrics and wrap dresses work well for adding balance.
  • Avoid clingy fabrics around the stomach area—opt for structured pieces instead.
  • Focus on drawing attention to your upper body with statement tops, off-the-shoulder styles, and structured jackets.
  • A-line skirts and wide-leg pants create a smooth silhouette.
  • Avoid overly tight bottoms that accentuate the hips disproportionately.
  • Create curves with peplum tops, ruffles, and belted styles.
  • Layering works wonders—try structured blazers or jackets to add definition.
  • Avoid straight, shapeless outfits that don’t add dimension.
  • Balance proportions with flowy skirts or wide-leg pants.
  • V-neck tops and wrap styles help soften the upper body.
  • Avoid shoulder pads or exaggerated shoulder details that make the upper body appear broader.
  • Fabric choices matter: Softer fabrics help create a more fluid silhouette, while structured fabrics add definition.
  • Patterns and colors: Darker shades tend to slim, while bold prints can accentuate certain areas.
  • Tailoring: If an outfit doesn’t fit quite right, don’t be afraid to tailor it for a custom look!
  • Versatility: Basics can be mixed and matched effortlessly, making outfit planning easier.
  • Longevity: High-quality basics last longer, reducing the need for constant replacements.
  • Effortless Style: Classic pieces always look put together, no matter how you style them.
  • White T-shirt: A well-fitted, high-quality cotton tee can be dressed up or down.
  • Classic button-up shirt: A crisp white or blue shirt works for formal and casual settings.
  • Neutral sweater: Go for timeless colors like beige, navy, or black.
  • Classic denim: A good pair of jeans (dark-wash or straight-leg) is always in style.
  • Tailored trousers: Perfect for work or smart-casual outings.
  • Black pants: A sleek black pair can be worn with anything.
  • Structured blazer: Instantly elevates any outfit.
  • Denim or leather jacket: Adds edge and style to casual looks.
  • Trench coat: A polished layer that works across seasons.
  • White sneakers: Go with everything and keep things casual yet stylish.
  • Black or nude heels: Timeless choices for formal wear.
  • Ankle boots: Great for transitional weather and adding sophistication.
  • Quality handbag: Opt for neutral colors like black, tan, or white.
  • Minimal jewelry: Simple gold or silver pieces for a touch of elegance.
  • Classic watch: Adds polish to any look.

    Your undertone is the subtle hue beneath the surface of your skin and falls into three main categories:
    • Warm Undertones: You have a golden, peachy, or yellow hue to your skin. Veins on your wrist appear greenish, and gold jewelry flatters you more than silver.
    • Cool Undertones: Your skin has pink, red, or bluish hues. Your veins look bluish or purple, and silver jewelry looks better on you.
    • Neutral Undertones: A mix of warm and cool elements. You can wear both gold and silver jewelry effortlessly.
    Warm Undertones
    Best Colors: Earthy and rich shades complement your skin.
    ✔️ Warm reds, oranges, yellows
    ✔️ Olive green, camel, mustard
    ✔️ Coral, warm beige, brown
    ✔️ Warm golds, bronzes
    Avoid: Cool pastels and icy tones, which can wash you out.

    Cool Undertones
    Best Colors: Jewel tones and icy hues flatter your complexion.
    ✔️ Cool blues, emerald green, navy
    ✔️ Deep purples, lavender, cool pinks
    ✔️ Crisp whites, cool grays, silver
    ✔️ Burgundy, ruby, sapphire
    Avoid: Too much yellow or orange-based hues, which can clash with cool undertones.

    Neutral Undertones
    Best Colors: Versatile shades from both spectrums.
    ✔️ Soft pastels, rose pinks, soft blues
    ✔️ Muted earthy tones—taupe, gray, beige
    ✔️ Balanced greens, plums, browns
    ✔️ Warm and cool jewel tones
    Avoid: Super extreme shades—too warm or too cool—without balance.
    ✅ Wear monochrome outfits in your best shades to create a cohesive look.
    ✅ Balance bold colors with neutrals to avoid an overwhelming effect.
    ✅ Use accessories—if a color doesn't suit you fully, incorporate it in bags, shoes, or earrings instead!
    ✅ Try makeup in flattering shades—lipstick, blush, and eyeshadow can further enhance your natural tones.
